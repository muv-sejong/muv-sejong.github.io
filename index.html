Research

Guidance-and-control-centered autonomy for marine unmanned systems.

MUV Lab studies the theoretical and algorithmic foundations of autonomous marine systems, with emphasis on guidance, nonlinear control, cooperative operation, and simulation-based verification under uncertainty, actuator constraints, and communication-limited environments.

01

Nonlinear Control for Autonomy

Lyapunov-based control, sliding-mode control, sampled-data control, actuator saturation, robustness, and stability analysis for autonomous unmanned systems.

02

Guidance and Path Following

Guidance laws, path-following control, trajectory tracking, waypoint missions, and 3D maneuvering for underwater and surface vehicles.

03

Cooperative Marine Autonomy

Formation control, cooperative path following, distributed coordination, and autonomous operation under delayed, intermittent, and low-rate underwater communication.

04

Mission-Level Simulation

6-DOF vehicle dynamics, digital twin simulation, mission scenario generation, performance evaluation, and verification of autonomy algorithms.
